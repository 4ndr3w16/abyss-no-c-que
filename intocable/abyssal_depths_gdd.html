01 — Creative Vision

Tone, Atmosphere & Philosophy

Abyssal Depths is built on a single foundational truth: the player's skill is the only currency that matters. Not levels. Not statistics. Not grinding. Every encounter, every corridor, every boss arena is a mechanical question — and the answer is mastery.

The world feels ancient beyond memory. It is the sediment of collapsed civilizations — royal crypts swallowed by biomechanical temples, cathedrals grown over with eldritch organisms, machine-gods lying broken beneath oceans of ash. The player is not a hero descending into darkness. They are a stranger in a world that has forgotten the concept of mercy.

Isolation. Curiosity. Dread. These are the three emotional pillars that govern every design decision — from the arc of a shadow on a distant corridor wall, to the way a dying boss's second phase music shifts from grandeur to desperation.

The bells no longer ring in the drowned towers. Something vast and patient has learned to speak through the silence between heartbeats. We called it hunger. The ancients built temples to it.

— Fragment recovered from the Ossuary of the Third Descent

The setting synthesizes medieval decay, eldritch horror, ancient arcane technology, and surreal architecture into a unified visual language. Ruined aqueducts carry luminescent fluid through caverns the size of cities. Biomechanical constructs crawl through collapsed throne rooms. Every environment tells a story of something that was once magnificent — and was devoured.

02 — Design Pillars

Core Pillars

Six interlocking design pillars govern every decision in Abyssal Depths. When conflict arises between systems, these pillars serve as the arbitrating authority — in this exact priority order.

I
Skill Supremacy

Victory is earned through mechanical understanding, not stat accumulation. Every system must reward execution and punish passivity.

II
Fluid Momentum

Combat and movement form a single continuous language. Interrupting momentum should always feel like a player choice, never a system failure.

III
Discovery Over Direction

Minimal handholding. The world reveals itself to those who explore it honestly. Secrets should feel earned, not scheduled.

IV
Oppressive Atmosphere

The world must feel dangerous, ancient, and unknowable. Every visual, audio, and environmental decision reinforces existential dread.

V
Risk & Reward

Every path forward has a cost and a prize. Players must make meaningful choices under pressure. Greed should sometimes be rewarded.

VI
Build Diversity

RPG progression must enable wildly different playstyles without trivializing difficulty. No build should be strictly correct.

03 — Combat Design

The Art of Controlled Violence

Combat in Abyssal Depths is a performance. Skilled players conduct fights like composers — building pressure, exploiting openings, chaining techniques across weapons and abilities in sequences that are both mechanically optimal and visually spectacular. The combo ranking system rewards aggression, variation, and damage avoidance simultaneously, creating constant incentive to push into danger.

Enemy AI is designed to punish passive play. Enemies read hesitation and escalate pressure. Bosses evolve their attack patterns mid-fight, demanding that players adapt — not simply endure. Frame-tight defensive mechanics separate good players from exceptional ones: a perfect parry at the edge of an attack window yields rewards that make the risk worth taking.

04 — Movement System

Fluid Traversal

Movement is not separate from combat — it is combat. Every traversal ability doubles as a combat tool, and every combat technique can be expressed through movement. The distinction between exploration and fighting dissolves in the hands of a skilled player.

Traversal abilities unlock progressively, true to metroidvania tradition — but each ability also opens up combat options that redefine how the player engages with the systems they already know. The air dash that bypasses a chasm also becomes a combo extender mid-fight. The wall jump that reaches a hidden ledge also repositions the player during a boss's vulnerability window.

Core Movement
  • Dash — ground and aerial, directional
  • Double jump with momentum preservation
  • Wall jump and wall cling
  • Slide — low-profile with hitbox reduction
  • Momentum preservation through air
  • Combat-integrated sprint system
Unlockable Traversal
  • Grappling hook — precise momentum swing
  • Air dash — zero-gravity burst direction
  • Void step — short-range blink through thin barriers
  • Abyssal anchor — rapid vertical descent
  • Chain grapple — multi-target sequence
  • Spectral sprint — brief phasing through enemies
05 — Dungeon Systems

Semi-Procedural Depths

Dungeons in Abyssal Depths occupy the space between handcrafted artistry and procedural unpredictability. The macro-structure of each region is carefully designed — its visual identity, narrative significance, faction, and boss are fixed. But within that structure, room configurations, enemy placements, hazard types, trap arrangements, and event triggers shift between runs.

This creates a dual effect: players develop genuine familiarity with a region's character and danger, while never feeling fully safe. Something is always different. The patrolling corrupted knight may take a different route. The collapse trap that ended the last run may not trigger the same way. A merchant might appear where a dead end once stood.

⚗
Curse & Corruption

Entering cursed zones applies stacking negative modifiers — reduced healing, increased enemy aggression, or reversed controls. Cleansing requires specific rituals or items.

🜏
Relics

Game-altering artifacts that dramatically modify mechanics. Some grant overwhelming power with severe drawbacks. Others reshape the ruleset of an entire run.

⚔
Elite Spawns

Randomly placed elite enemies appear with unique buffs and modified attack patterns. Killing them yields rare resources and upgrades — but they are not required.

🜁
Resource Extraction

Death risks losing carried loot. Players must weigh pushing deeper against returning to preserve gains. High-tier resources only drop in deep, dangerous zones.

🝰
World Events

Rare in-region events appear randomly — a collapsed merchant, a ritual in progress, a dormant ancient machine reactivating. These yield unique outcomes and lore.

☽
Nightmare Zones

Optional challenge rooms hidden behind obscure triggers. Nightmare difficulty with modified enemy behavior and unique reward pools for players seeking maximum challenge.

⬡
Treasure Vaults

High-risk locked rooms with powerful loot behind consecutive challenge encounters. Failure collapses the vault and scatters its contents into harder-to-reach positions.

✦
Imprisoned NPCs

Randomly placed imprisoned characters yield unique dialogue, merchant inventories, quests, or permanent upgrades when freed. Some carry critical lore fragments.

⚡
Dynamic Hazards

Environmental dangers shift per run — collapsing floors, acid floods, moving blade arrays, awakened traps. Positions and activation states are semi-randomized.

06 — RPG Progression

Depth Without Trivialization

Progression in Abyssal Depths must never become a substitute for skill. Every upgrade makes the player more expressive, not more invulnerable. New techniques, abilities, and tools open up combat possibilities. But enemies scale with the world — and the world is always dangerous.

Temporary dungeon upgrades layer over permanent character progression, creating a bifurcated system: the core character grows slowly and meaningfully, while dungeon runs offer fast, volatile power spikes that expire or carry risk. Relics from dungeons can permanently alter class traits if specific conditions are met — creating emergent build possibilities that are discovered, not prescribed.

Build diversity is enforced by design: equipment sets, relic combinations, and skill trees are built to create mutual exclusion and meaningful tradeoffs rather than universal optimal paths. A Crimson Executioner going all-in on hemorrhage chains plays nothing like one who takes the counter-redirect variant — and both are viable.

07 — Playable Classes

Five Combat Philosophies

Each class is a complete reimagining of how the game's systems are engaged — not a variation on a theme, but a fundamentally different relationship with combat, movement, resource management, and risk. Switching classes should feel like playing a different game.

Abyssal Knight
Void-Infused Swordmaster
I

The game's mechanical baseline — deceptively difficult to master. The Abyssal Knight excels at combo extensions through void cancels, adaptive offense that responds to enemy aggression shifts, and seamless integration of mobility into damage windows. Their void energy meter enables brief bursts of hyper-offensiveness when sustained pressure builds it. Designed to reward players who internalize the full combat system.

08 — Enemy Design

The Hostile Abyss

Every enemy faction in Abyssal Depths has a distinct visual identity, behavioral philosophy, and mechanical language. Encountering a new enemy type should require active study and adaptation — not pattern recognition from other games. The first encounter should feel dangerous. The tenth should feel like a mastered conversation.

⚔
Corrupted Royal Knights

Former honor guard, now vessels of void. Highly aggressive, disciplined attack patterns, shield-break mechanics. They remember how to fight — the void just removed the reluctance.

⚙
Biomechanical Monstrosities

Organic tissue fused with ancient machinery. Attack patterns mix mechanical predictability with organic unpredictability — learning which limb is which is part of the fight.

🜃
Ancient Machine Guardians

Pre-collapse automated sentinels. Precise, patient, telegraphed attacks that become overwhelming in coordination. Their logic can be learned — then exploited.

☽
Hollow Priests

Devotees of something that answered. Now empty vessels channeling eldritch signal. Field-modifier enemies that alter environmental rules — gravity, sound, visibility.

🦠
Eldritch Parasites

Swarm intelligence with hive coordination. Individually weak but tactically sophisticated. Kill the node or fight a thousand iterations of the same mistake.

⚗
Failed Experiments

The results of the ancients' ambition. Unpredictable, erratic, frequently dangerous to themselves. Their attack patterns are corrupted — expect the unexpected.

✦
Spectral Assassins

Phase between visibility states. Strike from angles that require environmental awareness. Defeating them requires learning the tells of their materialization pattern.

🌊
Giant Abyssal Predators

Something that lived at the bottom before there was a bottom. Arena-scale encounters with environmental repositioning requirements. Treated as mini-bosses even in common areas.

🜏
Broken Artificial Gods

The ancients' crowning achievement, now shattered. Pieces still function — dangerously. Puzzle-adjacent encounters requiring multi-part neutralization sequences.

⬡
Fanatical Machine-Cultists

Humans who merged willingly with the old infrastructure. Coordinated unit tactics, shared health pools, sacrifice abilities that empower their allies. Threatening at range and close.

09 — Boss Encounters

Cinematic Skill Checks

Bosses are not gatekeepers — they are arguments. Each one presents a mechanical thesis about a specific aspect of the combat system, and defeating them requires engaging with that argument honestly. There is no stat threshold that bypasses a boss's design. There is only understanding.

Every major boss has cinematic visual presentation tied directly to their lore, multi-phase transitions that feel earned rather than arbitrary, and a final phase that demands everything the player has learned about the fight be applied simultaneously.

The Sovereign Without Crown
First Warden of the Drowned Palace

Once the last king's personal executioner. Now void-consumed, their judicial greatsword leaves afterimages that persist as active hitboxes. Phase two collapses the arena floor, introducing a vertical combat plane. Tests: parry timing, aerial repositioning, reading multi-hit combos.

Vael, the Breathing Architecture
Living Temple — Biomechanical Colossus

A cathedral that became self-aware when the old machines infected its foundation stones. The arena is its body — walls breathe, floors convulse, pillars attack. Three phases: dormant geometry, partial awakening, full consciousness with global area attacks. Tests: environmental reading, dynamic positioning.

The Ashen Chorus
Four-Voice Hollow Priest Conclave

Four priests, each controlling a different field modifier — gravity, visibility, timing distortion, hitbox inversion. Alive together, they compound effects. Must be eliminated in a specific order to prevent resurrection. Tests: priority targeting, field modifier adaptation, split attention management.

SENTINEL-0, the First Prohibition
Ancient Machine Arbiter

A pre-collapse automated judicial AI in a body the size of a fortress gate. Attacks are perfectly telegraphed but require positional solutions rather than evasion. Phase three activates emergency protocols and abandons logic entirely. Tests: pattern decoding, spatial problem-solving, breaking away from learned responses.

Erethis, the Unfinished God
Final Experiment — Half-Born Deity

The ancients attempted to construct divinity. They almost succeeded. Erethis exists in two states simultaneously — physical and spectral — and attacks from both planes at once. The player must track presence across overlapping realities while maintaining offensive output. Tests: all combat systems simultaneously.

The Unmoored Bell
Abyssal Acoustic Predator

A deep-sea intelligence that migrated upward when the lower levels flooded with void. Attacks operate on sound wave physics — damage radiates outward from impact points. The arena fills with resonance pools that amplify or nullify damage. Tests: spatial awareness, unconventional hitbox reading, resource control under pressure.

10 — World Design

The Interconnected Ruin

The world of Abyssal Depths is a single, fully interconnected structure — the accumulated ruin of multiple civilizations that rose, collapsed, and were swallowed by whatever came before them. No region is isolated. Every zone connects to at least two others through hidden pathways, traversal-gated passages, or destructible walls.

Regions are defined not by biome but by the civilization that once inhabited them — each with a distinct architectural language, enemy philosophy, and environmental hazard type. The Drowned Palace is sunken royalty, half-submerged in void-tainted water. The Machina Ossuary is a graveyard of ancient automated systems, still partially operational. The Bleeding Spires are the remains of an order that worshipped something that answered. Each region tells its story entirely through what remains.

The map does not show what is below the map. It never has. Whatever built the Seventh Layer built it before the concept of cartography existed — before the concept of warning.

— Wall inscription, undeciphered orientation, Sublevel Archives
11 — Narrative Design

Environmental Storytelling

There is no narrator. There are no cutscenes longer than thirty seconds. There is no character who explains the lore. The world is the story, and the player constructs their understanding of it through active exploration and observation.

Lore delivery systems: murals in ruined throne rooms that depict events out of chronological order, cryptic dialogue from NPCs who refuse to explain themselves, item descriptions that reference events without context, environmental configurations that imply catastrophic histories, and rare hidden chambers containing dense fragments that reward obsessive exploration.

The narrative structure mirrors the world structure — fragmentary, non-linear, demanding active reconstruction. Players who want to understand what happened will need to find every piece. Players who simply want to fight through the darkness will have a complete emotional experience without ever knowing the names of the civilizations they're walking through.

12 — Presentation

Audio & Visual Direction

Every visual and audio decision must reinforce the same emotional truth: you are small, this place is ancient, and something enormous once lived here and left marks.

Visual Direction
  • Hand-drawn 2D environments with frame-by-frame animation
  • Dark gothic fantasy — heavy blacks, deep contrast, selective color emphasis
  • Massive environmental scale — structures that dwarf the player sprite
  • Atmospheric particles: fog, ash, rain, bioluminescent spores
  • Biomechanical architecture — organic growth over ancient machinery
  • Insect, deep-sea, and religious imagery fused in creature design
  • Hit feedback through screen shake, chromatic aberration, motion blur
  • Particle systems for every combat interaction — impacts, parries, dodges
  • Dynamic lighting that shifts with environmental state changes
Audio Direction
  • Dark ambient — sustained drone textures, resonant low-frequency base
  • Processed choir elements that suggest language without being intelligible
  • Industrial undertones — machinery, rust, pressurized release sounds
  • Melancholic orchestration — strings, brass, occasional solo woodwind
  • Minimalist tension tracks during exploration — space between sounds
  • Intense, percussive boss themes — escalating into final phase intensity
  • Dynamic music system — combat detection blends tracks in real time
  • Positional audio — enemy sounds telegraph attack direction
  • Distinct impact sounds per weapon type, hit surface, and enemy material
