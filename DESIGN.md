# The Art of Controlled Violence

Combat in Abyssal Depths is a performance. Skilled players conduct fights like composers — building pressure, exploiting openings, chaining techniques across weapons and abilities in sequences that are both mechanically optimal and visually spectacular. The combo ranking system rewards aggression, variation, and damage avoidance simultaneously, creating constant incentive to push into danger.

Enemy AI is designed to punish passive play. Enemies read hesitation and escalate pressure. Bosses evolve their attack patterns mid-fight, demanding that players adapt — not simply endure. Frame-tight defensive mechanics separate good players from exceptional ones: a perfect parry at the edge of an attack window yields rewards that make the risk worth taking.

## Movement System

### Fluid Traversal

Movement is not separate from combat — it is combat. Every traversal ability doubles as a combat tool, and every combat technique can be expressed through movement. The distinction between exploration and fighting dissolves in the hands of a skilled player.

Traversal abilities unlock progressively, true to metroidvania tradition — but each ability also opens up combat options that redefine how the player engages with the systems they already know. The air dash that bypasses a chasm also becomes a combo extender mid-fight. The wall jump that reaches a hidden ledge also repositions the player during a boss's vulnerability window.

### Core Movement

- Dash — ground and aerial, directional
- Double jump with momentum preservation
- Wall jump and wall cling
- Slide — low-profile with hitbox reduction
- Momentum preservation through air
- Combat-integrated sprint system

### Unlockable Traversal

- Grappling hook — precise momentum swing
- Air dash — zero-gravity burst direction
- Void step — short-range blink through thin barriers
- Abyssal anchor — rapid vertical descent
- Chain grapple — multi-target sequence
- Spectral sprint — brief phasing through enemies

## Dungeon Systems

### Semi-Procedural Depths

Dungeons in Abyssal Depths occupy the space between handcrafted artistry and procedural unpredictability. The macro-structure of each region is carefully designed — its visual identity, narrative significance, faction, and boss are fixed. But within that structure, room configurations, enemy placements, hazard types, trap arrangements, and event triggers shift between runs.

This creates a dual effect: players develop genuine familiarity with a region's character and danger, while never feeling fully safe. Something is always different. The patrolling corrupted knight may take a different route. The collapse trap that ended the last run may not trigger the same way. A merchant might appear where a dead end once stood.

## RPG Progression

### Depth Without Trivialization

Progression in Abyssal Depths must never become a substitute for skill. Every upgrade makes the player more expressive, not more invulnerable. New techniques, abilities, and tools open up combat possibilities. But enemies scale with the world — and the world is always dangerous.

Temporary dungeon upgrades layer over permanent character progression, creating a bifurcated system: the core character grows slowly and meaningfully, while dungeon runs offer fast, volatile power spikes that expire or carry risk. Relics from dungeons can permanently alter class traits if specific conditions are met — creating emergent build possibilities that are discovered, not prescribed.

Build diversity is enforced by design: equipment sets, relic combinations, and skill trees are built to create mutual exclusion and meaningful tradeoffs rather than universal optimal paths. A Crimson Executioner going all-in on hemorrhage chains plays nothing like one who takes the counter-redirect variant — and both are viable.

## Playable Classes

### Five Combat Philosophies

Each class is a complete reimagining of how the game's systems are engaged — not a variation on a theme, but a fundamentally different relationship with combat, movement, resource management, and risk. Switching classes should feel like playing a different game.

### Abyssal Knight — Void-Infused Swordmaster

**Tier I** — The game's mechanical baseline, deceptively difficult to master. The Abyssal Knight excels at combo extensions through void cancels, adaptive offense that responds to enemy aggression shifts, and seamless integration of mobility into damage windows. Their void energy meter enables brief bursts of hyper-offensiveness when sustained pressure builds it. Designed to reward players who internalize the full combat system.

- Balanced
- Combo Extension
- Void Energy
- Mobility Canceling
- Adaptive

### Crimson Executioner — Blood-Fueled Berserker

**Tier II** — Relentless, punishing, and unforgiving to play passively. Dual scythes enable devastating combo chains where the player's own HP becomes a resource — spending blood fuels stronger attacks, but overextension is lethal. The Executioner must maintain constant forward pressure or lose their hemorrhage stacks and momentum. Built for players who want pure aggression with real stakes.

- Extreme Aggression
- HP as Resource
- Hemorrhage Chains
- Execution Triggers
- High Risk/Reward

### Void Arcanist — Gravity & Ether Manipulator

**Tier III** — Fragile but capable of the highest damage ceiling in the game. The Arcanist reshapes the battlefield — gravity inversions suspend enemies, portals redirect projectiles, forbidden energy creates lingering hazard zones. Positioning and preparation define every encounter. Demands spatial intelligence and planning under pressure; mistakes are immediately fatal.

- Battlefield Control
- Gravity Manipulation
- Portal Mechanics
- Glass Cannon
- Forbidden Energy

### Mechanical Hunter — Gadgeteer & Hybrid Fighter

**Tier IV** — The most mobile class in the roster — constantly in motion, leveraging firearms, deployable traps, and transforming weapons that shift between melee and ranged configurations mid-combo. The Hunter turns the environment into an extension of their arsenal, using trap placement and gadget timing as core combat structure. Rewards creative improvisation and multi-tool mastery.

- Hybrid Melee/Ranged
- Transforming Weapons
- Trap Deployment
- High Mobility
- Gadget Systems

### Umbral Monk — Counter & Precision Master

**Tier V** — No weapon drawn until the enemy attacks. The Umbral Monk's entire combat philosophy is reactive precision — reading attack patterns, timing parries to the frame, breaking enemy stance through counter-chains, and punishing aggression with devastating counter-strikes. The widest parry window in the game, but the smallest error margin on everything else. For players who want combat to feel like a dialogue.

- Parry-Focused
- Counter Chains
- Stance Breaking
- Reactive Combat
- Pattern Reading

## Enemy Design

### The Hostile Abyss

Every enemy faction in Abyssal Depths has a distinct visual identity, behavioral philosophy, and mechanical language. Encountering a new enemy type should require active study and adaptation — not pattern recognition from other games. The first encounter should feel dangerous. The tenth should feel like a mastered conversation.

| Icon | Name | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| ⚔ | Corrupted Royal Knights | Former honor guard, now vessels of void. Highly aggressive, disciplined attack patterns, shield-break mechanics. They remember how to fight — the void just removed the reluctance. |
| ⚙ | Biomechanical Monstrosities | Organic tissue fused with ancient machinery. Attack patterns mix mechanical predictability with organic unpredictability — learning which limb is which is part of the fight. |
| 🜃 | Ancient Machine Guardians | Pre-collapse automated sentinels. Precise, patient, telegraphed attacks that become overwhelming in coordination. Their logic can be learned — then exploited. |

| 🦠 | Eldritch Parasites | Swarm intelligence with hive coordination. Individually weak but tactically sophisticated. Kill the node or fight a thousand iterations of the same mistake. |
| ⚗ | Failed Experiments | The results of the ancients' ambition. Unpredictable, erratic, frequently dangerous to themselves. Their attack patterns are corrupted — expect the unexpected. |
| ✦ | Spectral Assassins | Phase between visibility states. Strike from angles that require environmental awareness. Defeating them requires learning the tells of their materialization pattern. |
| 🌊 | Giant Abyssal Predators | Something that lived at the bottom before there was a bottom. Arena-scale encounters with environmental repositioning requirements. Treated as mini-bosses even in common areas. |

| ⬡ | Fanatical Machine-Cultists | Humans who merged willingly with the old infrastructure. Coordinated unit tactics, shared health pools, sacrifice abilities that empower their allies. Threatening at range and close. |
